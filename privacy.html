Privacy policy & terms

Sobermark for Android (app.sobermark) · Last updated 14 July 2026

Your privacy

Everything you enter in Sobermark stays on your phone. There is no account, no sign-up and no tracking. Sobermark does not collect your personal data or send it anywhere.

  • Data we collect: none. Your streak, check-ins, journal entries, cravings and settings are stored only on your device.
  • Backups are yours. The optional backup exports a file to a location you choose. We never see it.
  • Deletion. You can reset your progress or erase everything at any time from Settings → Delete all data. Uninstalling the app also removes all data.
  • Purchases. Subscriptions and one-time purchases are processed by Google Play. To validate purchases, our payment provider (RevenueCat) receives an anonymous purchase token from Google Play — never your name, email or anything you enter in the app.
  • No third-party analytics or ads.

Questions? Contact us at anthonycorby@gmail.com.

Terms of use

Sobermark is a personal self-help tool, provided as-is to support your own goals. Use it alongside, not instead of, professional care. By using the app you agree it is for personal, non-commercial use.

Payments are handled by Google Play. The yearly subscription includes a 7-day free trial and renews automatically unless cancelled at least 24 hours before the trial or period ends; manage or cancel anytime in Google Play. The lifetime purchase is a one-time payment with no renewals.

A note on your health

Sobermark is not medical advice, diagnosis or treatment, and it is not a substitute for professional help. If you are struggling with dependency, or stopping could affect your health, please speak to a doctor or a qualified professional first.

You are not alone

If things feel too heavy, reaching out is a strength, not a setback. Free, confidential support is there whenever you need it.

  • Find a helpline (worldwide): findahelpline.com
  • Samaritans (UK): call 116 123, free, 24/7
  • NHS advice (UK): call 111, or visit nhs.uk
  • In an emergency: call your local emergency number (999 UK, 911 US, 112 EU)

The UK lines are examples. Wherever you are, the worldwide directory above lists free, confidential helplines for your country.
